SDG attributes: A sustainability assessment framework for Brazilian ports

SDG 7SDG 8SDG 9SDG 11SDG 12SDG 13SDG 14SDG 17
Port sustainabilitySustainable Development Goals (SDGs)Sustainability indicatorsPort resilienceSustainable port developmentMaritime policy

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.marpol.2025.106841

Abstract

This study develops and applies a sustainability index tailored to the port sector, aligned with eight SDGs. The framework combines 20 thematic dimensions and 84 indicators, offering a quantitative assessment of port sustainability performance. Applied to six major Brazilian ports, it revealed strong results in partnerships (SDG 17) and responsible consumption (SDG 12), but gaps in climate action (SDG 13) and clean energy adoption (SDG 7). The model provides practical guidance for port authorities and policymakers to strengthen governance, resilience, and sustainability strategies.

A Bibliometric Analysis of Sustainable Development Goals in the Maritime Industry and Port Sector

Sustainable Development GoalsSustainabilityEnvironmental sustainabilityMaritime industryPorts

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.rsma.2023.103319

Abstract

This article conducts a comprehensive literature review on SDG-related research in the maritime and port industries. Using IRaMuTeQ and VOSviewer, it identifies key themes such as port–city models, circular economy, blue economy, and energy efficiency. Results show a strong European focus, reflecting the region’s academic prominence and extensive port network. Despite growth in publications, the study highlights a persistent gap in aligning port operations with the ambitions of the 2030 Agenda, indicating opportunities for further exploration in governance and policy.

Sustainable Practices in Brazilian Ports: Aligning with SDGs 9, 13, and 14

SDG 9SDG 13SDG 14
Sustainable Development GoalsInnovationClimate actionMarine lifePort sector

https://doi.org/10.24857/rgsa.v18n11-096

Abstract

This research assesses the implementation of SDG 14 in Brazilian ports through eight in-depth interviews. The findings highlight initiatives such as ISO 14001 certification, water quality monitoring, waste management, and environmental education programmes. Partnerships with academia and regulators and adherence to the UN Global Compact reinforce this progress. However, challenges remain in noise monitoring, biodiversity conservation, and the use of eDNA tools. The study recommends improved environmental reporting standards and the adoption of SMART targets to strengthen alignment with marine conservation goals.

Challenges and strategies for SDG 14 in Brazilian ports

SDG 14
Sustainable Development GoalsSDG 14SustainabilityBlue economyPorts

https://doi.org/10.7769/gesec.v15i12.4593

Abstract

This study analyses sustainability disclosures in reports from major Brazilian public and private ports. Evidence shows investments in research, innovation, and technology (SDG 9), actions to cut greenhouse gas emissions and promote energy transition (SDG 13), and limited initiatives related to marine protection (SDG 14). The findings highlight the port sector’s clear commitment to innovation and climate action, while also revealing a need to expand practices addressing biodiversity and marine ecosystems.

Photovoltaic electronic waste in Brazil: circular economy challenges, potential and obstacles

SDG 7SDG 9SDG 12SDG 13
E-WasteEnd-of-life photovoltaicSolar panelsRenewable energy

https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ssaho.2023.100456

Abstract

This article investigates sustainability practices reported by ten Brazilian port authorities managing 17 ports responsible for 76% of national cargo. The study reveals stronger focus on SDG 8 (decent work and economic growth), SDG 11 (sustainable cities and communities), and SDG 14 (life below water). Although the SDGs began to appear in port reports from 2018, by 2020 only four authorities still published such information. Overall, the study shows that the integration of SDGs in Brazilian port governance remains incipient and requires consolidation.

Sustainability practices for SDGs: a study of Brazilian ports

SDG 7SDG 8SDG 9SDG 11SDG 12SDG 13SDG 14
Sustainable development goalsSustainability reportEnvironmental practicesPorts

https://doi.org/10.1007/s10668-023-03126-y

Abstract

This article discusses the environmental impacts of end-of-life photovoltaic (PV) panels in Brazil. While PV technology is a mature and promising renewable energy source, discarded panels generate significant electronic waste containing metals and toxic materials. Beyond technological solutions for recycling, the study stresses the importance of regulatory frameworks and public policies to manage industrial waste effectively. It highlights the urgency of integrating circular economy principles to ensure that PV energy contributes to sustainability without creating new environmental burdens.
